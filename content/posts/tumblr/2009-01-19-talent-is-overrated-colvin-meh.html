Talent is Overrated (Colvin) - meh

Picked up this book at the PWM bookstore en route to BWI. Thought I was actually getting Outliers (which is the one I actually wanted to read) — but couldn’t remember the title at the time. Talent is Overrated is overrated, long winded, and lacks excitement:

Mozart and Tiger Woods practeded their entire lives? Their fathers were teachers? Practice from an early age makes people better at what they do?

Colvin could have just written that down in a page or two to save me the time reading 200-something pages hammering the same point home: practice makes you better.  Seriously, we know this.

Robert’s Amazon review sums it up pretty nicely.

Oh yeah, Malcom Gladwell is the guy who gave the excellent ‘Spagetti’ talk on TED.
